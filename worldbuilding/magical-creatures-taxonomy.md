# Magical Creatures & Ecosystem Taxonomy

## Purpose
This document establishes the foundation for **Weltina's Silverleaf Preserve** operations and the broader magical creature material economy. It defines what creatures exist, where they live, their conservation status, and their role in the ecosystem.

---

## Conservation Status Classifications

### **Critically Endangered** (Population < 50)
- **Immediate extinction risk**
- **Weltina's Priority 1:** Emergency breeding programs, habitat protection
- **Material Availability:** Extremely rare, only ethical alternatives accepted
- **Legal Status:** Total hunting ban in all kingdoms, possession requires permits

### **Endangered** (Population 50-500)  
- **High extinction risk without intervention**
- **Weltina's Priority 2:** Active conservation programs, population monitoring
- **Material Availability:** Very rare, strict ethical sourcing only
- **Legal Status:** Hunting banned, material trade heavily regulated

### **Vulnerable** (Population 500-2,000)
- **Moderate extinction risk**
- **Weltina's Priority 3:** Habitat protection, sustainable management
- **Material Availability:** Limited, ethical sourcing preferred
- **Legal Status:** Hunting permits required, trade monitored

### **Stable** (Population 2,000+)
- **Low extinction risk**  
- **Weltina's Priority 4:** Population monitoring, habitat maintenance
- **Material Availability:** Moderate, sustainable harvesting acceptable
- **Legal Status:** Regulated hunting seasons, standard trade rules

### **Abundant** (Population 10,000+)
- **No extinction risk**
- **Weltina's Priority 5:** Ecosystem balance maintenance
- **Material Availability:** Common, normal commercial harvesting
- **Legal Status:** Standard hunting regulations, open trade

## Major Creature Categories

### **Draconic Family**

#### **True Dragons** - Critically Endangered (12-15 worldwide)
- **Habitat:** Mountain peaks, volcanic caverns, ancient lairs
- **Lifespan:** 2,000+ years
- **Conservation Challenges:** Territorial conflicts with kingdoms, slow reproduction
- **Materials:** Scale sheds, claw sheaths, breath essence (ethically collected during molting)
- **Weltina's Approach:** Diplomatic agreements, lair protection, molt monitoring

#### **Drakes** - Vulnerable (400-800)
- **Habitat:** Mountain ranges, cliff dwellings
- **Lifespan:** 200-500 years  
- **Conservation Challenges:** Territory loss to mining operations
- **Materials:** Smaller scales, talon tips, fire glands
- **Weltina's Approach:** Mining permit negotiations, protected nesting areas

#### **Wyverns** - Stable (3,000+)
- **Habitat:** Forested mountains, hunting grounds
- **Lifespan:** 80-150 years
- **Conservation Status:** Stable but monitored
- **Materials:** Wing membrane, poison sacs, bone fragments
- **Weltina's Approach:** Sustainable hunting permits, breeding program support

### **Ethereal Family**

#### **Unicorns** - Endangered (80-120)
- **Habitat:** Ancient forests, sacred groves
- **Lifespan:** 800-1,200 years
- **Conservation Challenges:** Habitat destruction, poaching for horns
- **Materials:** Horn shavings (naturally shed), mane hair, hoof filings
- **Weltina's Approach:** Grove protection, anti-poaching patrols, ethical partnership

#### **Phoenixes** - Critically Endangered (8-12)
- **Habitat:** Volcanic regions, fire-aspected magical zones
- **Lifespan:** Immortal (rebirth cycle every 500-1,000 years)
- **Conservation Challenges:** Magical zone disruption, feather harvesting
- **Materials:** Molted feathers, ash from rebirth sites, tears (rare)
- **Weltina's Approach:** Rebirth site protection, feather collection agreements

#### **Celestial Stags** - Vulnerable (200-400)
- **Habitat:** Moonlit clearings, star-touched meadows  
- **Lifespan:** 300-600 years
- **Conservation Challenges:** Light pollution, magical disturbance
- **Materials:** Antler velvet, starlight essence, ethereal fur
- **Weltina's Approach:** Dark sky preservation, meditation site protection

### **Aquatic Family**

#### **Leviathans** - Critically Endangered (3-5)
- **Habitat:** Deep ocean trenches, underwater caverns
- **Lifespan:** 3,000+ years
- **Conservation Challenges:** Ocean pollution, deep sea exploration
- **Materials:** Scale fragments, echo-song recordings, pressure oils
- **Weltina's Approach:** Ocean conservation partnerships, deep sea protection

#### **Merfolk Allies** - Stable (8,000-12,000)
- **Habitat:** Coral reefs, coastal waters
- **Lifespan:** 200-400 years
- **Conservation Status:** Political entity, not conserved but partnered
- **Materials:** Gifted pearls, worked coral, blessed seawater
- **Weltina's Approach:** Diplomatic trade agreements, reef protection

### **Elemental Family**

#### **Fire Salamanders** - Abundant (15,000+)
- **Habitat:** Volcanic regions, geothermal vents
- **Lifespan:** 50-100 years
- **Conservation Status:** Stable, fast reproduction
- **Materials:** Heat-resistant skin, internal flame essence, salamander oil
- **Weltina's Approach:** Sustainable population management

#### **Ice Spirits** - Vulnerable (600-1,200)
- **Habitat:** Glacial regions, permanent ice formations
- **Lifespan:** 400-800 years (tied to ice formation persistence)
- **Conservation Challenges:** Climate change, ice mining
- **Materials:** Ice crystals, frost essence, chill vapor
- **Weltina's Approach:** Glacier protection, climate monitoring

### **Forest Family**

#### **Dryads** - Endangered (150-300)
- **Habitat:** Ancient trees, old growth forests
- **Lifespan:** Tied to host tree (500-2,000 years)
- **Conservation Challenges:** Deforestation, tree diseases
- **Materials:** Blessed bark, leaf essence, root fragments (given voluntarily)
- **Weltina's Approach:** Forest preservation, tree health programs

#### **Treants** - Vulnerable (80-200)
- **Habitat:** Deep forests, grove centers
- **Lifespan:** 1,000-3,000 years
- **Conservation Challenges:** Forest fragmentation, fire damage
- **Materials:** Heartwood splinters, amber tears, seasonal growth
- **Weltina's Approach:** Corridor creation, fire prevention programs
