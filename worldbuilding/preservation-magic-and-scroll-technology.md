## Scroll Technology

### What Scrolls Are

In Nubimancy, scrolls are **multi-purpose physical documents** that serve simultaneously as communication artifacts, legal instruments, information records, and â€” in some specialist cases â€” stored magical effects. The physical format is deeply trusted and has been standard for centuries because scrolls cannot be remotely intercepted or altered after sealing in the way that aethernet transmissions technically can be. A sealed scroll is also inherently bounded: it exists in one place, can be witnessed, and can carry a binding enchantment that triggers with the reader's contact.

**Common scroll types by purpose:**

**Physical materials:**

Common trade-route scrolls use **bark-derived paper** as the lowest cost option â€” produced from Sylvaran-sourced tree bark processed in Aurumvale mills. ## Preservation Requirements for Scrolls

### Why Scrolls Degrade

Scrolls face two distinct degradation mechanisms that operate at different rates and require different interventions:

**1. Physical decay** â€” the substrate itself deteriorates. Vellum is vulnerable to moisture fluctuation (expansion and contraction cycles crack the surface and crack the ink). Bark paper is vulnerable to heat (brittles and yellows within weeks in desert conditions). Crystal fiber parchment resists both but is vulnerable to strong magical interference fields (unusual, but relevant near high-tier portals or Delyra's workshop zones).

**2. Magical information decay** â€” the enchantment or magical binding carried by a scroll dissipates over time. This can render a spell scroll inert, dissolve a binding contract (catastrophic for legal enforceability), or degrade an authentication seal to the point where it can no longer be verified. Magical information decay occurs independently of physical condition â€” a scroll can be physically perfect but magically dead. This is the more commercially significant failure mode for high-value scrolls.

**Typical unpreserved lifespan by scroll type:**

| Type | Substrate | Physical Lifespan (unpreserved) | Magical Integrity (unpreserved) |
|------|-----------|--------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Basic correspondence | Bark paper | 1â€“3 years (temperate); weeks (humid/desert) | N/A (no enchantment) |
| Standard vellum contract | Vellum | 10â€“20 years (dry storage); 1â€“2 years (transit abuse) | Authentication seal degrades: 1â€“2 years |
| Diplomatic scroll | Vellum + sealing wax | 15â€“30 years (dry storage) | Kingdom seal: 2â€“5 years standard; enchanted seal: up to 10 years |
| Crystal fiber archival | Crystal fiber | 100+ years (undamaged) | Enchantments stable: 10â€“50 years depending on tier |

## Scrying Technology and Intelligence Gathering

### What Scrying Provides

Scrying in a route planning context is **active intelligence gathering** â€” using magical observation to collect current-state data about conditions along route segments. This is distinct from the passive broadcast infrastructure that supports Rini's tournament coverage. Route planning scrying is query-based: a network operator points a scrying instrument at a defined zone and returns a condition report.

**Types of intelligence gathered via scrying:**

| Intelligence Type | What It Captures | Route Planning Use |
|-------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Weather and atmospheric conditions** | Current weather conditions, temperature extremes, storm fronts within 48â€“72 hours | Departure timing, waypoint scheduling, porter condition assessment |
| **Route hazard assessment** | Physical obstructions (rock falls, flood damage, bridge failures), known threat presence, unusual creature activity | Segment risk rating, route avoidance, guard escalation decision |
| **Creature migration patterns** | Mass movement of creatures through travel corridors | Timing decisions for high-risk segments; dovetails with Weltina's warden data subscriptions |
| **Caravan position verification** | Confirmation of friendly caravan positions for convoy coordination | Convoy scheduling, inter-squad coordination on multi-squad routes |
| **Threat actor intelligence** | Bandit camp activity, territorial disputes relevant to route segments | Threat rating adjustment, guard pricing, insurance rate inputs |
| **Portal gate operational status** | Whether a portal gate is online, currently occupied, or closed | Portal-integrated routing decisions (relevant to Bran's hybrid routes) |

**Accuracy and reliability:**  
Scrying accuracy varies with range, atmospheric magical interference, and equipment tier. Short-range scrying (under 100 km) achieves 85â€“95% accuracy on weather and physical conditions. Long-range scrying (200â€“500 km) drops to 65â€“80% accuracy and is more susceptible to interference. Creature and threat intelligence accuracy is inherently lower (50â€“75%) because mobile targets can move between the scrying observation and the caravan's transit of the same segment. Bran's route planning system applies confidence intervals to all scrying-sourced data and does not route solely on a single scrying report for high-risk segments.

**Refresh frequency:**  
Standard commercial scrying provides a **daily report cadence** for active route segments. Premium providers offer **twice-daily or on-demand queries** for specific segments. During active caravan operations, Bran's system is configured to trigger a fresh scrying query for the next 48 hours of route at each waypoint check-in. This means a caravan squad leaving a waypoint has scrying data that is at most 24 hours old for the next segment.

### Scrying as Route Planning Input

Bran's route planning system integrates scrying data at three levels:

**1. Pre-departure route selection:**  
Before a caravan squad departs a major hub, the route planning system consolidates available scrying data across all candidate route segments and produces a **risk-weighted route recommendation**. Each segment receives a composite score from weather risk, hazard presence, threat actor activity, and creature migration status. The system flags any segment scoring above the threshold as requiring captain approval before inclusion in the confirmed route.

**2. In-transit segment updates:**  
At each waypoint check-in, the route planning system triggers a scrying refresh for the next 48 hours. If a segment that was low-risk on departure has elevated since, the captain receives an **alert scroll** (drafted by Bran's hub intelligence staff from the scrying report) and a proposed route amendment. For life-threatening escalations (active armed conflict, bridgeable flood, known creature herd blocking), the captain has authority to deviate autonomously; the amendment is logged back to the system on the next check-in.

**3. Historical scrying data archives:**  
Each completed scrying report is archived with the corresponding route segment, date, and weather/season code. Over time this archive becomes a highly valuable **seasonal pattern database** â€” allowing the route planning system to predict risk levels for route segments without requiring a current scrying query. Bran's historical archive, which he has been building since Caravans of Fortune's founding, now covers approximately 85% of active route segments with at least 2 full years of seasonal records. This is one of the named competitive advantages in his business overview and is not easily replicable by newer competitors.

**Intelligence consulting revenue:**  
The scrying archive and real-time intelligence access are the foundation of Bran's ~10% intelligence and consulting revenue stream. Paying clients include:
- **Merchant guilds** purchasing route intelligence reports (format: 1-week outlook for a named route) â€” typically 30â€“80 AC per report

- **Art of Facts (Delyra)** purchasing regular transit-route intelligence for component sourcing routes â€” covered under Caravans of Fortune's standing partnership agreement with Art of Facts

The intelligence service is deliberately arms-length from the caravan operation. Reports are formatted to be useful to competitors using different carriers â€” this protects Bran from accusations of carrier bias and makes the intelligence service commercially credible as a standalone product.

Rush processing involves hand-carrying the scroll on the next departing squad regardless of squad capacity constraints, and may invoke the portal-accelerated option even for scrolls that would not normally qualify. Rush Processing commands a 300â€“600% premium over base price and is volume-limited (each hub maintains a maximum daily rush allocation to protect standard service quality).

### Route Intelligence Distribution

**Daily intelligence cycle for caravan squads:**  
Each of Bran's twelve caravan squads receives a **Route Intelligence Brief** at every waypoint check-in. During active operations, this brief covers: current scrying data for the next 3 segments, any threat alerts issued since the last brief, migration corridor status from Weltina's warden data feed (where applicable), and portal gate operational status for any portal-integrated segments on the forward route.

| Distribution method | Use case | Pros | Limitations |
|---------------------|----------|------|-------------|
| **Physical scroll (pre-prepared)** | Standard waypoint briefs; forward-staged at waypoints before squad arrival | Permanent record; captain can annotate; works without aethernet access | Must be pre-staged; can't reflect intelligence from the last 12 hours before pickup |
| **Aethernet update** | Urgent alerts, real-time deviation requests, threat escalations | Near-instant; reaches squads with aethernet access instantly | Squads in aethernet dead zones (Underhelm, remote Siroccane, some Sylvaran routes) cannot receive |
| **Crystal array relay** | Emergency-only; life-safety escalations | Real-time voice; most reliable for immediate decision | 500 km range limits; array infrastructure required at squad location; not available in transit between waypoints |

**Scroll-based alert priority system:**  
Pre-prepared route intelligence scrolls carry a **color-code seal** visible without opening: 
- **White seal:** Routine brief, no changes from previous report
- **Yellow seal:** Advisory update â€” elevated weather risk or mild route condition change; captain to review
- **Orange seal:** Significant change â€” route segment rating has risen; captain review required before next departure
- **Red seal:** Threat alert â€” captain must read and confirm receipt by return acknowledgment scroll before transit of affected segment

The red-seal acknowledgment requirement is a legal protection for Caravans of Fortune: it documents that the captain was informed of a known risk before choosing to proceed. In the event of an incident on a red-sealed segment, the acknowledgment scroll is material to insurance claim adjudication.

**Update frequency during active operations:**  
- At each waypoint: fresh brief pre-staged or aethernet-delivered
- Midpoint check (for segments >5 days): aethernet if available, otherwise no update until next waypoint
- Deviation log: captain logs any autonomous route deviation taken since last waypoint; these feed back to the route planning system and update the historical scrying archive with human-observed conditions
